Setting up the Telegram bridge

Manus supports webhook-based integrations. You connect a Telegram bot to Manus via the agent's API endpoint. Every message you send the bot becomes an instruction. Manus processes it, runs the workflow, and replies — with a file, a result, or an approval request before taking any high-stakes action.

1

Create a Telegram bot

Message @BotFather in Telegram and run /newbot. Copy the API token it generates.

2

Create a Manus workflow with a webhook trigger

In Manus, create a new workflow. Set the trigger type to Webhook / API. Copy the incoming webhook URL it provides.

3

Point your bot at Manus

Use Telegram's setWebhook API to route your bot's incoming messages to the Manus webhook URL. Every message you send now triggers your workflow.

4

Connect your tools

Inside Manus, connect Gmail, Google Calendar, LinkedIn, and Claude Cowork via their native OAuth or API integrations.

5

Configure your approval gate

For irreversible or public actions, set Manus to send a Telegram preview and wait for your "Confirm" before executing.

Security First

Restrict your Telegram bot to respond only to your own chat ID. Add a sender ID check as the very first step in every Manus workflow: if sender_id != YOUR_ID → stop and ignore. This ensures nobody else can trigger your agent even if they find the bot username.

02 — Presentations

Creating Presentations on Command

One of Manus's most powerful capabilities for IT professionals. Describe the deck you need — audience, purpose, structure, tone — and Manus produces a fully structured PowerPoint or Google Slides file with slide titles, content, and speaker notes. What used to take three hours of slide-wrestling now takes the time it takes to type a paragraph on your phone.

You → Manus: Create a 10-slide presentation on cloud infrastructure modernisation for a CTO audience. Cover: current-state pain points, a microservices migration roadmap, a 3-agent automation architecture, and a summary ROI slide. Clean dark enterprise theme. Deliver as PPTX. Manus → You: ✅ Deck ready. 10 slides + speaker notes. [📎 Cloud_Modernisation_v1.pptx] Reply "Revise slide 5" or "Add a risk slide" to iterate.

What to include in your prompt

  • Target audience — Manus adjusts depth, language, and structure accordingly
  • Slide count and the key sections you need covered
  • Specific frameworks, data points, or architecture patterns to reference
  • Colour palette or branding if applicable
  • Tone: executive summary, technical deep-dive, sales pitch, or internal update
  • Output format: PPTX, Google Slides, or PDF

Manus doesn't just fill slides — it reasons about narrative structure. It knows an executive deck opens with the business pain, not the solution. A technical deck needs the architecture diagram before the implementation roadmap.

Iterating via Telegram

Manus maintains context across the conversation, so you can refine your deck in a natural back-and-forth — exactly like briefing a designer, but at typing speed from your phone.

You: Strengthen the ROI slide. Add a 3-year NPV model assuming $500K implementation cost and 35% reduction in manual ops overhead. Manus: Updated. ROI slide now includes: Year 1: –$500K | Year 2: +$175K | Year 3: +$350K 3-year NPV at 8% discount rate: +$25K [📎 Cloud_Modernisation_v2.pptx]
03 — Video Content

For AI-generated video with an avatar, Manus integrates with tools like HeyGen or Synthesia via API — submitting your script and returning a rendered video link directly to your Telegram.

Manus: Script ready. Hook: "Most tech transformations don't fail at the start — they fail at the handover. Here's why." [📎 LinkedIn_Script_90s.docx] Reply "Generate as HeyGen video" to produce an AI avatar version.

04 — LinkedIn

LinkedIn Outreach and Content Posting

Manus handles two distinct LinkedIn workflows: personalised outreach DMs and connection requests, and content publishing — posts, carousels, and long-form articles. Both require your explicit approval before anything goes live or gets sent, protecting your professional reputation at all times.

Personalised outreach

Whether you're prospecting for consulting work, building partnerships, or growing your network, Manus researches the prospect, drafts a personalised first message under 120 words, and queues it for your review before sending.

You: Draft a LinkedIn DM to a VP Engineering at a mid-size SaaS company. My angle: I help engineering teams cut deployment cycle time using agentic CI/CD automation. Under 110 words. No pitch in the first message. CTA: a 20-minute conversation. Manus: Draft ready: "Hi [Name], your recent post on deployment bottlenecks resonated — we've been seeing the same pattern across engineering teams at scale. I've been working on agentic automation that's cut release cycle times by 40–60% for similar orgs. Would love to hear how your team is approaching it. Open for a quick 20-minute chat next week?" Reply "Send" to submit or "Edit: [changes]" to refine.

Posting content

Technical insights, lessons from the field, opinion pieces — Manus drafts, formats, and on your approval publishes directly to your LinkedIn profile via the LinkedIn API.

You: Write a LinkedIn post: 3 things nobody tells you before leading your first AI implementation. First-person, honest tone. Hook in line 1. End with a question to drive comments. Max 200 words. Manus: Draft ready. Hook: "I led my first AI project with full confidence. I was wrong about almost everything." [Preview — 196 words] Reply "Post now" or "Schedule: Thursday 8am"

Approval Gate — Always Active

Manus will never send a LinkedIn message or publish a post without your explicit "Send" or "Post now" confirmation in Telegram. This is a non-negotiable gate. Your public presence is always under your control.

Show me the first 5 for review before sending the full batch. Manus: 5 draft previews ready for your review. 06 — Calendar

Booking Calendar Invites and Meetings

Manus connects to Google Calendar and creates, updates, and cancels events — sending invites to all participants. Before proposing a time, it checks your calendar for conflicts, so it will never double-book you or schedule over an existing commitment.

You: Book a 45-minute technical review with the client team next Tuesday between 10am and 2pm. Add a Google Meet link. Title: "API Integration — Architecture Review". Send the invite to client@company.com and copy me. Manus: Tuesday 11:00–11:45 AM is free on your calendar. Event: "API Integration — Architecture Review" Date: Tuesday [date] · 11:00 AM Attendees: client@company.com + you Meet link: [auto-generated] Reply "Confirm" to send the invite.

Intelligent scheduling behaviours

  • Reads your existing events to avoid all conflicts before suggesting any slot
  • Respects blackout windows you define — "no meetings before 9am", "Friday afternoons free"
  • Auto-generates Zoom, Google Meet, or Teams links based on your default preference
  • Sets reminders at your preferred intervals — e.g. 15 minutes and 24 hours before
  • Suggests reschedule options if an existing conflict appears on that day
07 — Claude Cowork Tasks

Creating Tasks in Claude Cowork

Claude Cowork is an AI-assisted task and project workspace. Manus bridges Telegram and Cowork — when you give it an instruction, it simultaneously executes it and creates a corresponding task record, giving you a full log of what's been delegated, what's in progress, and what needs your review.

You: Create a Cowork task: "Prepare cloud migration deck for client kickoff". Priority: High. Due: this Friday. Add context: deck should cover current architecture, migration phases, risks, and a go-live timeline. Assign to me. Manus: ✅ Task created in Claude Cowork. Title: "Prepare cloud migration deck for client kickoff" Priority: High | Due: Friday [date] Context notes attached. Reply "Start now" and I'll begin building the deck immediately.

Querying your tasks from Telegram

Pull your open task list at any time directly from Telegram. Manus surfaces what's open by priority and flags anything sitting in your approval queue.

You: What are my open high-priority tasks in Cowork? Manus: 🔴 3 high-priority tasks open: 1. Cloud migration deck — due Friday 2. Client proposal draft — due Thursday 3. Infrastructure audit report — due tomorrow Want me to start on any of these now?
08 — Daily Reminders

Your Morning Briefing — Every Day at 9 AM

Configure Manus to push a structured daily briefing to your Telegram every morning — covering your schedule, open high-priority tasks, items pending your approval, and a suggested first action. This single automation saves most IT professionals 20–30 minutes of inbox-checking and context-switching every single morning.

Setting up the briefing automation

Create a scheduled Manus workflow set to run at your chosen wake-up time. Configure it to pull from Google Calendar (today's events), Claude Cowork (open tasks by priority), your Telegram approval queue (pending items), and any flags left over from the previous session.

Customise It to Your Style

You control the format entirely. Want it shorter? Tell Manus: "Keep the morning briefing to 5 bullet points max." Want weather added? Done. Want the suggested first action to always surface your highest-value item? Define that logic in your system prompt once and it applies every day automatically.

Manus never completes a financial transaction autonomously. For any booking requiring payment, it initiates the process, shows you the final checkout summary, and requires your "Confirm" before proceeding. You enter your payment details directly. Your card data never passes through the AI layer.

11 — Best Practices

Tips, Limits, and Making It Yours

Manus is remarkably capable, but its output quality is a direct function of the context you give it. Here's what separates a serviceable AI assistant from one that genuinely runs your operations.

Write a master system prompt

The single highest-leverage action you can take is writing a detailed system prompt that tells Manus who you are and how you work. Include your name, role, industry, preferred communication style, meeting availability rules, default output formats, and standing rules — for example, "always use plain text in emails", "never schedule meetings on Friday afternoons", or "keep LinkedIn posts under 200 words." Set it once and it applies to every interaction, permanently.

Use the approval gate deliberately

Auto-execute for low-stakes and reversible actions. Require approval for irreversible or public ones. This gives you full speed on the 80% that doesn't need a second look, and full control over the 20% that does.

⚡

Auto-Execute

Drafts, files, calendar lookups, task creation, research and summarisation.

🔒

Require Approval

Sending emails, LinkedIn posts, calendar invites, any action touching a third party.

🛑

Never Delegate

Payment credentials, passwords, confidential client data, legally binding commitments.

Know the limits

  • Manus is only as capable as its connected integrations — if a tool isn't linked via API, it can't act on it
  • Real-time data like live flight prices requires Manus's web browsing mode to be active
  • Complex multi-step tasks may take a few minutes — let it run rather than interrupting mid-process
  • Always review anything going to an external audience — Manus gets the 80% right, your judgement completes the final 20%
  • The more standing context you give it via system prompt, the less it needs to ask for clarification on every task

The best Manus setups don't try to automate everything at once. Identify the 10 recurring tasks that consume the most time and delegate those first. Master the workflow. Then expand from there.
